My Approach to Cyber Behaviour

Studying Abnormal Psychology and Cyber Behaviour has given me a new lens through which to view online interactions and personal profiles. I now recognize that a digital footprint is not just a collection of data, but a psychological profile that can be exploited by threat actors for social engineering, phishing, and other cybercrimes. This understanding goes beyond technical vulnerabilities and focuses on the human element, which is often the weakest link in any security chain. This perspective has made me more vigilant about my own online identity and has deepened my appreciation for how personality traits, cyber-addictive behavior, and social norms in cyberspace can be leveraged for both malicious and defensive purposes. This knowledge directly informs my approach to cybersecurity, allowing me to build more resilient defenses that account for human nature.

My Study Methods

My study methods are designed for long-term retention and practical application. I start by creating a concise summary of new concepts in my notes, followed by a detailed mind map to visualize the connections between topics. For more technical subjects, I use tools like PowerPoint to create diagrams and flowcharts, making complex systems easier to understand. I also use plenty of screenshots and small code snippets to document every step of a process, ensuring I can reproduce it later. This structured approach helps me not just memorize information but truly master it.
